# Synthea Data-Quality Audit for Clinical Co-Pilot

**Date:** 2026-04-30
**Scope:** Evaluating whether Synthea-generated data can replace the hand-built fixture bundle (`agent/src/copilot/fixtures.py`) as the seed data for the agent demo + eval suite.

---

## TL;DR

Synthea will get you ~70% of what the fixtures provide: solid longitudinal structured data (encounters, conditions, meds, vitals, labs, allergies) with real US-Core FHIR shapes — but it will **not** produce the rich free-text narrative the demo and several eval cases depend on, and it has **no concept of a clinician's daily panel** (UC-1). Plan to keep a small set of hand-crafted notes/adversarial fixtures alongside Synthea data, and synthesize the care-team / panel grouping at import time.

---

From `agent/src/copilot/fixtures.py` and `agent/src/copilot/tools.py`, the agent reads:

| Observation (laboratory) | `get_recent_labs` | code.display, valueQuantity |
| Encounter | `get_recent_encounters` | class.code, period.start/end, **reasonCode.coding.display**, serviceProvider.display |
| ServiceRequest | `get_recent_orders` | code.display, status, **reasonCode**, requester.display |
| DiagnosticReport (radiology) | `get_imaging_results` | **conclusion** (free text) |
| MedicationAdministration | `get_medication_administrations` | status, **statusReason**, dosage.text, performer |

The bolded fields are the ones with clinical *judgment* in them — and they are the load-bearing surfaces for verification/citation.

## What Synthea generates (R4 + US Core IG enabled)

**Structured resources — full coverage, lifetime history:**
Patient, Encounter, Condition, Observation (vitals + labs), MedicationRequest, MedicationAdministration, Procedure, Immunization, AllergyIntolerance, CarePlan, Goal, ImagingStudy, DiagnosticReport, DocumentReference, CareTeam, Practitioner, PractitionerRole, Organization, Location, Provenance, Claim, Coverage, ExplanationOfBenefit.

Generated by ~500 disease-progression modules from birth to current age — so longitudinal encounter history (years of visits, chronic-disease progression, hospitalizations) is one of Synthea's strengths.

## Gap-by-gap audit vs. agent expectations

### 1. Free-text clinical notes (DocumentReference) — **MAJOR GAP**

- Synthea *does* emit DocumentReference and a base64 attachment when US Core is on, but the contents are **template-rendered structured summaries** (the same skeleton as the text-exporter sample: `MEDICATIONS: …`, `CONDITIONS: …`, bullet lists), **not** physician prose.
- You will not get fixture-quality narratives like *"Patient hypotensive at 03:14, BP 90/60. 250 mL NS bolus administered per protocol. BP recovered to 112/70 by 04:00."* That is a hand-authored shape Synthea has no module for.
- **Impact:** UC-2 ("what happened overnight") and any eval case that asks the agent to *quote* a note loses its grounding. Source-attribution evals against narrative degrade to attribution-against-templated-section-headers.

### 2. Radiology-style DiagnosticReport.conclusion — **MAJOR GAP**

- Synthea emits DiagnosticReport mainly as a *bundle* of lab Observations. The `conclusion` field for radiology (`"Right lower lobe airspace opacity consistent with pneumonia"`) is **not generated** in any rich form. ImagingStudy gives you DICOM-style metadata, not a radiologist read.
- **Impact:** `get_imaging_results` returns empty/boilerplate conclusions. Linda Park's pneumonia-on-CXR eval case won't survive a Synthea swap as-is.

### 3. Observation.note (per-vital annotation) — **GAP**

- Vitals come out as bare `valueQuantity`/`valueString` + timestamp. No `note[]` array. The fixture's *"Hypotensive event; bolus given per protocol"* tag on a 90/60 reading is fixture-only.
- **Impact:** The "explain the BP nadir" reasoning the agent does on Eduardo currently has both the value *and* the annotation; with Synthea, only the value.

### 4. MedicationAdministration with `status=not-done` + clinical reasoning — **GAP**

- Synthea models prescriptions, not "RN held the dose this morning because BP was 90/60 and Cr was 1.8." `statusReason` populated with hold rationale is not part of Synthea's behavior.
- **Impact:** UC-2's signature "lisinopril held" example needs a hand-crafted MedicationAdministration on top of the Synthea import.

### 5. Past encounters / longitudinal history — **STRENGTH**

- This is what Synthea is best at: a 67-year-old patient will have decades of wellness visits, plus admissions/ED visits triggered by their disease modules, with `reasonCode` SNOMED-coded.
- **Caveat:** Synthea doesn't model rapid-response / crash-cart / overnight floor events. Encounters tend to be wellness, ambulatory, ED, inpatient — not 4-hour-window in-hospital subevents.

### 6. Care-team grouping ("Dr. Patel's panel of 5 patients on Floor 4 South") — **HARD GAP**

- Synthea's `CareTeam` is *per-patient* — the providers caring for **that** patient. It does not produce a clinician's panel (the inverse mapping: provider → list of patients).
- Synthea also won't pin five generated patients to the same Practitioner unless you explicitly post-process: assign one Practitioner ID across the panel, or build a sidecar `practitioner_patient_panel` table the `get_my_patient_list` tool reads.
- **Impact:** UC-1 ("triage my list this morning") requires a panel construct the agent does not get for free.

### 7. MedicationRequest.dosageInstruction — **OK with caveat**

- Populated and reasonable (e.g., "10 mg by mouth daily"). But Synthea will *not* produce your adversarial *missing-dose* MedicationRequest — keep that hand-crafted.

### 8. Adversarial fixtures (poisoned note, missing dose) — **KEEP HAND-BUILT**

- Synthea won't generate prompt-injection payloads or under-specified orders. `EVAL.md`'s adversarial-injection-002 and missing-dose cases must remain authored.

### 9. Temporal density — **GAP for "last 24 hours" framing**

- Synthea timestamps cluster around discrete encounter dates. Ten observations + meds + a procedure all share one encounter timestamp. The `_hours_window(24)` filter will mostly return either everything from the most recent encounter or nothing. UC-2's overnight 03:14 → 04:00 → 06:00 minute-by-minute texture has no Synthea analog.

### 10. Identifier stability — **OPERATIONAL RISK**

- Already noted in `EVAL.md:581`: Synthea regenerates UUIDs every run. You must pin one canonical export and treat it as a fixture (commit the bundle to the repo or to a deterministic seed).

## Recommended hybrid approach

1. **Use Synthea for the chronic-disease floor.** Generate ~50 patients, then pick 4 with desired conditions (CHF, post-op, pneumonia, syncope w/u) as the panel.
2. **Post-process the bundles before import:** rewrite each panel patient's attending `Practitioner`/`PractitionerRole` reference to a single canonical ID so `get_my_patient_list` can resolve the panel via FHIR rather than a constant.
3. **Layer hand-authored "today" data on top:** the rapid-response Encounter, the held-MedicationAdministration, the two narrative DocumentReferences, the radiology conclusion, the BP-with-note annotation, the missing-dose MedReq, the poisoned-note. These stay as hand-crafted FHIR resources POSTed after the Synthea import.
4. **Pin the generated bundle.** Commit one Synthea export as `agent/eval/cases/synthea-snapshot.json` and treat regeneration as a deliberate version bump.
5. **Update `EVAL.md`** to mark which cases depend on hand-authored layers (UC-2 narrative quoting, adversarial cases) so a Synthea re-import alone can never silently break them.

This keeps the FHIR-write+read path realistic (the demo claim in `ARCHITECTURE.md:92`), gives the eval suite real longitudinal data to ground past-history queries, and preserves the high-signal narrative surfaces the verification layer is designed around.
