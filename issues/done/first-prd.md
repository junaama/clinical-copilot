# PRD — Standalone Clinical Co-Pilot Portal

## Problem Statement

The clinician using the OpenEMR Clinical Co-Pilot today has to enter through OpenEMR itself: log in to the PHP UI, navigate to a specific patient's chart, then launch the Co-Pilot from a floating sidebar inside that chart. The Co-Pilot is bound to that one patient for the entire conversation — every question, every tool call, every audit row is pinned to whichever chart the clinician happened to be standing in when they opened the panel.

This breaks how clinicians actually think and round. A hospitalist with twelve patients on their service does not think "let me open Hayes' chart and ask about Hayes" — they think "who do I need to see first today?" and then "ok, tell me about Hayes" and then "and why is Cooper trending up?" all in one continuous flow. They want one conversation, multiple patients, switching freely.

The current design also forces a patient picker on the clinician before they can start working: open the chart, find the chart, click into the chart, then click the launcher. That is three clicks of friction in front of a tool that needs to be in front of a clinician in the ninety seconds between rooms.

## Solution

The Co-Pilot becomes a standalone, full-screen application that the clinician logs into directly using their OpenEMR credentials. After login they land immediately on their CareTeam panel — every patient assigned to them, listed and ready — with a chat surface alongside it. There is no patient-picker step.

Patient context is established by what the clinician says, not by where they entered. Saying "tell me about Hayes" resolves Hayes against the clinician's CareTeam roster and focuses the conversation there. Saying "and why is Cooper up so high?" resolves Cooper, switches focus, and keeps Hayes in scope for back-references. Every patient the clinician mentions in a conversation is added to a per-conversation registry so they can be referred back to without paying another resolution round-trip.

The agent picks its own tools — granular reads (meds, labs, vitals, encounters) and composite workflow tools (per-patient brief, panel triage, antibiotic stewardship, etc.) — guided by an advisory classifier that hints at the likely workflow but never constrains the surface. Every tool call passes through a CareTeam gate that re-validates authorization at the call site.

The clinician has multiple conversations available in a sidebar. Old threads are continuable; new threads start with the same empty CareTeam panel. Titles are auto-generated by a Haiku-class summarizer after the first turn so the sidebar reads like a chat-app, not a list of UUIDs.

Auth uses SMART-on-FHIR standalone launch with `launch/user` + `user/*.rs` scopes against OpenEMR's existing OAuth2 server. The access token never leaves the agent backend (BFF pattern) — the browser holds an HttpOnly session cookie. Token, session, and OAuth state are persisted in the agent's Postgres alongside the LangGraph checkpointer.

## User Stories

1. As a hospitalist arriving for morning rounds, I want to open the Co-Pilot URL and log in with my existing OpenEMR username and password, so that I do not have to remember a separate credential.

2. As a hospitalist who has just logged in, I want to land directly on my CareTeam panel without first being asked to pick a patient, so that I save the three clicks of friction the existing chart-launch flow imposes.

3. As a hospitalist looking at my CareTeam panel, I want to see every patient I am responsible for, so that I can scan the list and decide who to look at first.

4. As a hospitalist looking at my CareTeam panel, I want to click any patient and immediately see a 24-hour brief on them in the chat, so that I do not have to type "give me a brief on X" myself for the most common action.

5. As a hospitalist in an active conversation, I want to type a name like "tell me about Hayes" and have the agent resolve who I mean against my CareTeam roster, so that I do not have to copy a patient ID from anywhere.

6. As a hospitalist who mentions an ambiguous name, I want the agent to ask "John A. born 1965 or John A. born 1972?" so that I can disambiguate in the same conversational flow rather than through a picker.

7. As a hospitalist who has named several patients in a conversation, I want to refer back to any of them ("remind me what Hayes' last A1c was") without paying another resolution round-trip, so that conversation feels like talking to a colleague who remembers context.

8. As a hospitalist who is rounding, I want to switch focus between patients within one conversation ("now Cooper… and Smith… back to Hayes"), so that one rounding session is one conversation rather than five.

9. As a hospitalist asking "who do I need to see first?", I want the agent to surface a ranked list of my CareTeam panel sorted by clinical signal, so that I can prioritize my morning.

10. As a hospitalist asking a single targeted question ("is Hayes still on broad-spectrum antibiotics?"), I want the agent to fetch only what it needs and answer fast, so that I am not paying for a full brief when I only wanted one fact.

11. As a hospitalist preparing for a family meeting, I want the agent to surface the diagnosis story, prognosis, and treatment plan rather than raw vitals, so that I am not summarizing tables on the fly.

12. As a hospitalist coming on cross-cover, I want the agent to orient me on patients I have never met, so that I can pick up the service quickly.

13. As a hospitalist who has touched a chart before, I want the agent to tell me what changed since I last looked, so that I do not re-read what I already know.

14. As a hospitalist asking "why is this happening?", I want the agent to chain through the data and reason rather than just dumping everything, so that I get a hypothesis I can react to.

15. As a hospitalist asking about a patient who is not on my CareTeam, I want the agent to say "I don't see them on your panel" rather than refuse with "access denied," so that I am not made aware of patients I do not have a relationship with.

16. As a hospitalist mid-conversation, I want every claim the agent makes to be linked to the FHIR resource it came from, so that I can verify a statement against the source record.

17. As a hospitalist whose access token expires mid-conversation, I want the conversation to keep working without my having to log back in, so that token rotation is invisible.

18. As a hospitalist with several past conversations, I want a sidebar listing my conversation history, so that I can return to a thread from earlier in the day.

19. As a hospitalist looking at the conversation sidebar, I want each entry to show a meaningful auto-generated title (based on the first turn) and the patients it touched, so that I can find a past thread without opening every one.

20. As a hospitalist starting fresh, I want a "new conversation" button that takes me back to an empty CareTeam panel, so that I can start a new topic without polluting an existing thread.

21. As a hospitalist reopening an old conversation, I want to be able to keep typing rather than seeing a frozen log, so that conversations are continuable across the day.

22. As a system administrator, I want every tool the agent calls to be logged with the user, the patient touched, the gate decision, and the FHIR refs fetched, so that I can audit the system after the fact.

23. As a system administrator, I want the agent to run with a non-admin user during demos and evals, so that the CareTeam gate is exercised against real authorization boundaries rather than admin's god-mode access.

24. As a security reviewer, I want the access token to never reach the browser, so that an XSS in the chat UI cannot lift a `user/*.rs` token.

25. As a security reviewer, I want OAuth state, session cookies, and FHIR tokens persisted in encrypted columns in the agent's Postgres, so that the at-rest story is defensible.

26. As an LLM evaluator, I want every conversation, every workflow decision, and every gate decision visible in audit rows, so that I can correlate eval failures against system behavior.

27. As a developer, I want the existing EHR-launch flow (chart sidebar embed) to keep working, so that we have a fallback path if the standalone shell needs a hot fix.

28. As a developer, I want clicking a patient in the panel and typing "give me a brief on X" to produce identical behavior, so that I have one code path to test instead of two.

29. As a developer, I want OAuth, session, conversation, and audit logic each behind a single deep-module interface, so that we can test them in isolation without spinning up the whole graph.

30. As a developer, I want to roll the system out without changes to the LangGraph checkpointer schema, so that the existing per-turn audit pattern continues to work unchanged in week 1.

## Implementation Decisions

### Authentication & session

- Add a SMART-on-FHIR **standalone launch** flow alongside the existing EHR-launch flow. Standalone uses scopes `openid fhirUser launch/user offline_access user/Patient.rs user/Observation.rs user/Condition.rs user/MedicationRequest.rs user/MedicationAdministration.rs user/Encounter.rs user/AllergyIntolerance.rs user/DocumentReference.rs user/DiagnosticReport.rs user/ServiceRequest.rs user/CareTeam.rs user/Practitioner.rs`.
- Introduce a second OAuth client `copilot-standalone` registered in `oauth_clients` with `client_role='user'`, separate redirect URI, separate secret. The existing EHR-launch client (`copilot-launcher`, `client_role='patient'`) stays registered.
- New endpoint pair on the agent backend: `GET /auth/login` (kicks off PKCE redirect to OpenEMR's authorize endpoint) and `GET /auth/smart/callback` (exchanges code, mints session, sets cookie, redirects to copilot-ui root).
- Existing `/smart/launch` and `/smart/callback` endpoints remain wired and continue to serve the EHR-launch path.
- Token holding follows the **BFF pattern**: the access token, refresh token, and id_token live only in the agent backend's Postgres; the browser carries an opaque session cookie (`copilot_session`, HttpOnly, Secure, SameSite=None).
- User identity is resolved by parsing the `fhirUser` claim from the id_token (`Practitioner/<uuid>`), then mapping `users.uuid` → `users.id` in the OpenEMR database. Resolved once per login and cached on the session row.
- Token refresh runs server-side. Conversations survive access-token expiry without user action.
- A `GET /me` endpoint reports `{ user_id, display_name, panel_count }` so copilot-ui can detect whether a session exists at boot and route accordingly.
- A `POST /auth/logout` endpoint revokes the session row and clears the cookie.

### CareTeam authorization gate

- A new `CareTeamGate` module exposes one function: `assert_authorized(user_id, patient_id) -> AuthDecision`. The decision is one of `allowed`, `careteam_denied`, `no_active_patient`, `patient_context_mismatch`.
- The gate queries the `care_team` and `care_team_provider` tables (or the FHIR `CareTeam.participant` resource) to determine whether `user_id` participates in `patient_id`'s care team.
- Every patient-data tool calls the gate before fetching. Composite workflow tools route through the same gate per nested call.
- A `list_panel(user_id) -> list[ResolvedPatient]` function on the same module returns the user's CareTeam roster, used by the empty-state UI.
- Admin users bypass the gate by virtue of `acl_check_user('admin')` returning true; this is a deliberate week-1 backdoor that is documented.

### Patient resolution & registry

- A new tool `resolve_patient(name: str, dob: str | None, mrn_tail: str | None)` returns one of four statuses: `resolved` (single match), `ambiguous` (multiple matches with disambiguators), `not_found` (no matches on the user's CareTeam *or* anywhere — collapsed deliberately for privacy), or `clarify` (input too sparse to search).
- Search is **CareTeam-prefiltered** — the resolver never queries patients outside `list_panel(user_id)`. This makes "exists but you don't have access" indistinguishable from "doesn't exist."
- Resolution is cache-hit-idempotent: calling `resolve_patient("Hayes")` twice in one conversation returns the cached result the second time but still writes an audit row.
- The conversation state grows a `resolved_patients: dict[pid, ResolvedPatient]` registry that accumulates monotonically and a `focus_pid: str | None` that points at the most recently resolved patient.
- The registry is rendered into the LLM's system message every turn: *"patients identified this conversation: Hayes Sarah (42), Cooper Jane (87). Current focus: Hayes Sarah."*
- The registry is persisted as part of `CoPilotState` via the existing Postgres-backed LangGraph checkpointer.

### Tool surface & routing

- The classifier is **demoted from a routing gate to a system-prompt hint**. It still emits `{ workflow_id, confidence }` and the result is logged in the audit row, but it does not gate the tool surface.
- The graph collapses `triage_node` and `agent_node` into one node. Workflow-specific behavior comes from (a) which composite tool the LLM picks and (b) which synthesis prompt is selected after tools have run.
- The full tool surface bound to the LLM has 21 tools: 13 existing granular reads, `resolve_patient`, and 7 composite workflow tools.
- Composite workflow tools, all of which fan out their internal FHIR calls in parallel:
  - `run_panel_triage()` — covers W-1.
  - `run_per_patient_brief(patient_id)` — covers W-2 and W-3 (different synthesis prompt).
  - `run_cross_cover_onboarding(patient_id)` — covers W-4 and W-5 (different synthesis prompt).
  - `run_consult_orientation(patient_id, domain)` — covers W-8.
  - `run_recent_changes(patient_id, since)` — covers W-9.
  - `run_panel_med_safety()` — covers W-10.
  - `run_abx_stewardship(patient_id)` — covers W-11.
- W-6 (causal trace) and W-7 (targeted drill) deliberately have no composite tool. The LLM uses granular reads guided by the synthesis prompt.
- Synthesis prompt selection happens after tool execution, keyed by the classifier's `workflow_id`. There are nine distinct synthesis prompts (W-1, W-2, W-3, W-4, W-5, W-8, W-9, W-10, W-11) plus a default for W-6, W-7, and `unclear`.

### Conversation lifecycle

- A new `copilot_conversation` Postgres table holds `(id, user_id, title, last_focus_pid, created_at, updated_at, archived_at)`. `id` is the same string as the LangGraph `thread_id`.
- Conversations are listed for the sidebar via `GET /conversations`, scoped to the authenticated user, ordered by `updated_at DESC`, excluding archived rows.
- Creating a new conversation hits `POST /conversations` and returns a fresh `conversation_id`; the client navigates to `/c/<id>`.
- Reopening an old conversation hits `GET /c/<id>` (via copilot-ui routing); the agent reloads the LangGraph checkpoint, which restores the registry and focus.
- Conversations are continuable on reopen. Old turns are preserved by the checkpointer; new turns append.
- Titles are generated by a **Haiku-class summarizer** invoked once after the first turn completes. The summarizer takes the first user message and the first assistant response and emits a short title (≤60 chars). Until the summarizer returns, the title is the truncated first user message.
- Archive UI is deferred; the column exists but is not exposed in week 1.

### Empty state & click-to-brief

- copilot-ui's empty state for any conversation with no turns yet renders a full-screen CareTeam panel via `GET /panel` (returns the user's roster with display fields: name, DOB, last admission, room).
- Clicking a panel entry **injects a synthetic human message** into the conversation: `"Give me a brief on <patient name>."` The message is rendered in the chat history as a normal user message and goes through the full graph (classifier → agent → verifier).
- The synthetic message resolves the patient (cache-hit since the panel already populated the registry) and the LLM picks `run_per_patient_brief`. End-to-end latency is one FHIR fan-out plus one synthesis call.
- This is the same code path as the user typing the message themselves. There is no separate deterministic-prefetch path.

### Audit

- The existing **per-turn audit shape is preserved unchanged**. One row per turn, written to JSONL when no DSN, to a Postgres `agent_audit` table when DSN is set.
- The `extra` jsonb field is extended with two new keys: `gate_decisions: list[str]` (one entry per tool call in the turn, ordered) and `denied_count: int`. Existing keys remain.
- No per-call audit rows. No FK from call to turn. No new audit table.
- Free text (user prompts, assistant responses) continues to NOT be written to the audit row, per existing discipline.

### Browser-side handoff

- Standalone callback sets `Set-Cookie: copilot_session=<sid>; HttpOnly; Secure; SameSite=None; Path=/` and redirects to copilot-ui's root with no query parameters.
- copilot-ui boots, calls `GET /me` with `credentials: 'include'`. If 200, render the panel; if 401, render the login button which links to `/auth/login` on the agent backend.
- All subsequent API calls from copilot-ui include the cookie; CORS is configured `allow_credentials=True` (already set).
- The existing URL-parameter handoff (`?conversation_id=…`) on `/smart/callback` for the EHR-launch path is preserved and unchanged.

### Demo user seeding

- A new seed script creates an OpenEMR `users` row for `dr_smith` (provider role, non-admin), a corresponding FHIR `Practitioner` resource, and `CareTeam` participation rows assigning `dr_smith` to roughly half of the Synthea patient set.
- Admin remains seeded and remains a CareTeam-bypass for debugging.
- The demo flow logs in as `dr_smith` so the gate is exercised against real authorization boundaries.

### Schema changes

Four new tables in the agent's Postgres (same DSN as the LangGraph checkpointer):

- `copilot_oauth_launch_state` — short-lived PKCE/state row keyed by `state`, with `expires_at` for lazy cleanup.
- `copilot_session` — keyed by `session_id`, holds `oe_user_id`, `current_conversation_id`, `expires_at`, plus encrypted token bundle reference.
- `copilot_token_bundle` — keyed by `conversation_id`, holds encrypted access/refresh/id tokens, scope, issuer, expiry.
- `copilot_conversation` — sidebar metadata, keyed by `id` (= thread_id), holds title, last focus pid, timestamps.

Tokens at rest are encrypted at the application layer (AES-GCM, key from env). The encryption module is shared between session and token tables.

### API contracts (agent backend)

- `GET /auth/login` — 302 to OpenEMR authorize endpoint with PKCE.
- `GET /auth/smart/callback?code=&state=` — exchanges code, mints session, sets cookie, 302 to copilot-ui root.
- `POST /auth/logout` — revokes session, clears cookie.
- `GET /me` — authenticated user info or 401.
- `GET /panel` — CareTeam roster for the authenticated user.
- `GET /conversations` — sidebar list.
- `POST /conversations` — create new conversation, returns `{ id }`.
- `POST /chat` — existing endpoint, now requires session cookie instead of taking `conversation_id` as a free body field.
- `GET /smart/launch`, `GET /smart/callback` — preserved for EHR-launch fallback.

### OpenEMR side changes

- `CopilotClientRegistration::ensureRegistered()` is extended to register *both* clients (`copilot-launcher` for EHR launch and `copilot-standalone` for standalone). The standalone client uses `client_role='user'` and the user-scoped scope string.
- A static `scopeStringStandalone()` returns the standalone scopes.
- The redirect URI for `copilot-standalone` is the agent backend's `/auth/smart/callback`, not copilot-ui's `/callback`.
- A small SQL seed file establishes `dr_smith` and the CareTeam membership rows.

### Frontend changes (copilot-ui)

- A new full-screen `AppShell` replaces the floating `Launcher` + `AgentPanel` layout. The existing `AgentPanel` is reused as the conversation surface.
- A new `ConversationSidebar` lists threads, with a "+ new" button.
- A new `PanelView` renders the CareTeam roster as the empty state.
- A new `useSession` hook abstracts the `/me` boot check and login redirect.
- `api/client.ts` is updated to `credentials: 'include'` and to drop the URL-param `conversation_id` plumbing in the standalone path.
- The existing SMART-launch URL parsing in `App.tsx` is preserved and runs only when copilot-ui is loaded inside the EHR-launch iframe.
- Routing is added: `/`, `/c/:conversation_id`, `/login`. Default route redirects to most recent conversation or panel.

## Testing Decisions

A good test in this codebase exercises **external behavior** of a deep module — its inputs and outputs as visible to a caller — and does not assert on internal implementation details (which functions were called, which intermediate state values existed, which order private operations happened in). The agent codebase already follows this pattern: `test_smart.py` walks the OAuth state machine through real-shaped fixtures rather than mocking individual helpers; `test_audit.py` writes events and reads back the file rather than poking at internal write functions.

### Modules to test

- **`SessionGateway`** — happy path login (login URL → callback with code → session minted → cookie issued), expired state, invalid code, refresh token rotation, logout revocation. Prior art: `agent/tests/test_smart.py` (existing EHR-launch state machine tests).

- **`CareTeamGate`** — `allowed` for in-team pid, `careteam_denied` for out-of-team pid, `no_active_patient` for empty pid, admin-bypass behavior, panel listing returns only in-team patients. Prior art: `agent/tests/test_patient_context_guard.py` (existing patient-mismatch enforcement tests; the new tests replace this file's logic).

- **`resolve_patient` resolver** — single-match resolution, ambiguous-match path with disambiguators, not-found path (CareTeam pre-filter collapses with no-match), idempotent cache hit on second call. Prior art: `agent/tests/test_chat_contract.py` for the tool-result envelope shape.

- **`ConversationRegistry`** — list scoped to user, create returns id, get returns full row, update_focus persists, archive excluded from list, Haiku title summarization called exactly once after first turn. Prior art: none for this module — establishes new pattern.

- **Audit `extra.gate_decisions`** — turn with all-allowed calls produces `[allowed, allowed, ...]`, turn with one denial produces correct array and `denied_count=1`, turn with no tool calls produces empty array. Prior art: `agent/tests/test_audit.py` (existing JSONL audit tests).

- **Graph integration: synthetic click-to-brief** — injecting `"Give me a brief on Hayes."` produces the same audit row shape and same final block shape as a typed message. Prior art: `agent/tests/test_graph_smoke.py`.

- **Graph integration: registry survives across turns** — turn 1 resolves Hayes, turn 2 resolves Cooper, turn 3 references "Hayes' labs" without re-resolving (audit shows cache hit, FHIR call uses Hayes' pid). Prior art: none — establishes new pattern.

- **OpenEMR-side `CopilotClientRegistration`** — both clients are registered idempotently, secrets are persisted to `globals`, scope strings match expected. Prior art: existing PHPUnit pattern in `tests/Tests/`.

- **End-to-end OAuth flow** — login redirect, callback with real OpenEMR auth server, session cookie set, `/me` returns user. Prior art: `agent/scripts/seed/oauth_login.py` for the existing fixture-mode token acquisition.

### Modules deliberately not unit-tested at first

- The 7 new composite workflow tools are exercised through eval cases (`agent/evals/`) rather than unit tests, because their value is in the LLM-guided behavior on real prompts, not in deterministic input-output mapping.
- The synthesis prompts are not unit-testable in any meaningful way; they are evaluated through the existing eval harness against golden conversations.

## Out of Scope

- Multi-tenancy across OpenEMR instances. The agent talks to one OpenEMR.
- Mobile-specific UI. The shell is full-screen desktop-first.
- Conversation archive and delete UI. The column exists but is not exposed.
- Conversation export. No PDF, no CSV, no email.
- Voice input or voice output.
- Real-time push to the panel (e.g., new lab alerts). Panel is fetched on boot/refresh.
- Patient write-back. The agent does not order, prescribe, or chart; nothing the agent does mutates the EHR.
- Custom CareTeam editing UI. CareTeam membership comes from seeded data and OpenEMR's existing care-team management; the Co-Pilot reads it but does not modify it.
- Replacing the existing EHR-launch path. The chart-sidebar embed continues to work alongside the standalone shell.
- Per-tool-call audit rows or a new audit table. Per-turn rows with summarized gate decisions are sufficient for week 1.
- Switching the OAuth flow to support social login or non-OpenEMR identity providers.
- Token-scope rotation per resolved patient (mint a new token narrowed to one pid per turn). Authorization is enforced at the application layer, not the OAuth layer.

## Further Notes

- The classifier-as-advisory design is reversible: a single config flag can re-enable it as a tool-set gate if evals show the LLM picks badly with a 21-tool surface.
- The audit `extra.gate_decisions` array is a forward-compatible upgrade path. If AgentForge's later weeks ask for per-call queryability, splitting the array into a child table is a non-breaking migration.
- Encrypting tokens at rest is overkill for fixture-mode dev but cheap to wire in once and worth it for the audit deliverable.
- The deliberate collapse of "no access" and "not found" in `resolve_patient` is a privacy decision, not an accident. A clinician with a typo and a clinician querying outside their team see the same message; the latter has no signal that the patient even exists.
- The `dr_smith` seed is the difference between a CareTeam gate that *appears* to work (admin sees everything) and a CareTeam gate whose denials show up in the demo. AgentForge weights authorization heavily, and the demo video showing "Maria Chen isn't on Dr. Smith's team — the agent can't find her, but admin sees her instantly" is the cheapest 15 seconds of evidence available.
- Haiku-summarized titles are non-blocking: the sidebar renders the truncated first message until Haiku returns, then swaps in the better title. A failed Haiku call leaves the truncated title in place and is not retried.
- The existing EHR-launch flow being preserved means the system has two ways in. This is a small ongoing complexity tax that buys a real fallback if the standalone shell needs to be hot-fixed.
- Reusing the existing per-turn audit shape was a deliberate choice to avoid schema churn during week 1. The shape was designed for the EHR-launch model where one conversation = one patient; with multi-patient conversations, the per-turn `patient_id` field now refers to the *focus_pid at end of turn*, which is documented behavior, not a bug.
